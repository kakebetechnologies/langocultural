who we are?

Preserving Lango Culture,
Uniting Our People

The Lango Cultural Institution (LCI) is the traditional authority representing the Lango people of mid-northern Uganda. Under the leadership of Won Nyaci Eng. Dr. Moses Michael Odongo Okune, we serve millions of people across multiple districts including Lira, Apac, Alebtong, Dokolo, Amolatar, Oyam, Kole, Otuke, and Kwania. With 149 registered clans, our mandate encompasses preserving Lango cultural heritage, promoting unity among our people, and supporting sustainable community development through education, cultural programs, and strategic partnerships with government and development organizations.

Cultural Preservation

Safeguarding our traditional dances
like Okeme and Abuda, protecting
sacred sites, and documenting our
rich cultural heritage.

Education Programs

Supporting our youth through
scholarship initiatives and
educational partnerships for
community advancement.

Community Unity

Bringing together 149 clans
across nine districts to promote
peace, development, and Lango
cultural identity.
